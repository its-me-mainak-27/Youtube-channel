    • Shantiniketan was established in 1901 by the Nobel Laureate and poet Rabindranath Tagore. The name translates to "The Abode of Peace" in Sanskrit. The area began as a meditation center in 1863, founded by Tagore's father, Maharishi Debendranath. Rabindranath Tagore wrote many of his literary works at Santiniketan. Shantiniketan is a residential school and art center based on ancient Indian traditions. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The school's objective is to make learning a natural part of life. The first step towards this goal is to establish a sense of oneness with nature The town is known for its literary and artistic heritage, with the notable figures being Rabindranath Tagore and Nandalal Bose. In addition, the Visva Bharati also serves as Shantiniketan's cultural center.

    • The Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha is one of the oldest Hindu temples in the world. It is a sacred shrine dedicated to the deity Jagannath, an incarnation of Lord Vishnu. The main deities in the temple are Lord Jagannath, Lord Balabhadra, and Goddess Subhadra.Jagannath Temple in Puri, Odisha, is a revered temple where Lord Krishna is worshipped as Lord Jagannath. There are several unexplained mysteries associated with the temple, such as the direction of the flag, the wooden idols, absence of a shadow, the Mahaprasad served to Lord Jagannath, sound of waves, and more.
